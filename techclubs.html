Our Tech Clubs

At Moi University, we host a variety of technology-focused clubs that cater to different interests and career paths. Each club offers unique opportunities for learning, networking, and hands-on experience in various tech fields.

IEEE Student Branch

The IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) Student Branch at Moi University is a technical community dedicated to advancing technology for humanity. Our branch provides members with opportunities to network with peers and professionals, develop technical and professional skills, and work on projects that make a difference.

We organize workshops, technical talks, competitions, and social events that help members stay current with emerging technologies and build lasting connections in the engineering field.

Google Developer Group (GDG)

Google Developer Group (GDG) Moi University is a community of developers interested in Google's developer technology. From Android, Web, and Google Cloud Platform, to Angular, Machine Learning, and more, we cover a wide range of technologies.

Our mission is to create a space where developers can network, learn, and grow together. We organize tech talks, workshops, hackathons, and study jams that help members enhance their skills and stay updated with the latest technologies.

Mech-Design Club

The Mechanical Design Club at Moi University brings together students passionate about mechanical engineering, CAD design, and innovative problem-solving. We focus on practical applications of mechanical engineering principles through hands-on projects and design challenges.

Our club provides access to industry-standard design software, 3D printing facilities, and opportunities to work on real-world engineering projects. Members develop crucial skills in mechanical design, prototyping, and project management.

WAAW Foundation Moi Chapter

WAAW Foundation Moi University Chapter is a student-led organization dedicated to empowering African women in STEM through mentorship, outreach programs, and hands-on training. As part of the global Working to Advance STEM Education for African Women (WAAW) Foundation, our chapter actively engages in outreach programs to secondary schools, mentoring and inspiring young girls to pursue STEM careers.

We also provide technical training, leadership development, and advocacy initiatives to bridge the gender gap in STEM. Additionally, the global WAAW Foundation offers scholarships to university girls pursuing STEM-related courses. Join us in shaping the future.
